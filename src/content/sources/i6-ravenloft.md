The module known by its product code introduced the character and setting that
would eventually grow into an entire campaign world. It presents a self-contained
haunted valley ruled by an undead lord, reached by travellers who become trapped
once the surrounding fog closes behind them.

Two design ideas from this adventure proved unusually durable. The first is a
card-drawing ritual that reshuffles the location of key items, the villain's
weakness, and the adventure's final confrontation each time the module is run, so
no two playings unfold identically. The second is a sympathetic, tragic
motivation for the antagonist rather than a purely monstrous one — a ruler cursed
by his own past choices, defined as much by longing as by menace.

The valley, its lord, and its gothic tone were later expanded into a full
setting of many such cursed realms. This module remains the origin point that
every later treatment traces back to.
