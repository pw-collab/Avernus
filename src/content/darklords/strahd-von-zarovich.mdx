Strahd von Zarovich is the vampire who rules the fog-bound valley of
[Barovia](/domains/barovia), and the first of the setting's Darklords — the
template for every cursed ruler who followed. He is not a mindless monster but a
proud, cultured, and dangerously intelligent lord: a former conqueror who won his
domain in war and has held it for centuries as both its master and its prisoner.

## The lord of the valley

Strahd governs from a castle set high above the valley, and his reach extends
across the whole land. He commands wolves and lesser undead, moves unseen through
his own halls, and treats the arrival of outsiders as a diversion — guests to be
received with cold courtesy before they become prey. He is charming when it suits
him and merciless when it does not, and he regards the valley's people as
possessions rather than subjects.

## A ruler who cannot leave

Whatever power Strahd holds inside the valley, its border of Mist holds him too.
He is the master of a cage he cannot open, and much of his cruelty is the boredom
and bitterness of an immortal who has run out of everything except appetite. This
is the pattern the whole setting inherits from him: a Darklord is defined less by
strength than by a punishment fitted exactly to the crime that created them.

<SpoilerWrapper level="dm-only" summary="Strahd's origin and torment">

The heart of Strahd's story is a betrayal of his own making. Consumed by envy of
a younger man's youth and by desire for a woman who did not want him, he made a
ruinous bargain for immortality and shed family blood to seize what he wanted. The
bargain gave him deathless power and took everything the power was meant to win:
the woman was lost to him in the same moment, and the valley closed around him as
a prison. Ever since, a woman bearing her face is reborn in each generation —
most recently [Ireena Kolyana](/npcs/ireena-kolyana) — and Strahd is compelled to
pursue her again, and to fail again. He is his own jailer, and the recurrence of
that lost love is the lever a story about him should keep pulling.

</SpoilerWrapper>

## Running Strahd

He works best played as a patient, theatrical antagonist who already holds every
advantage: he watches, toys, invites, and withdraws, letting inevitability do the
frightening rather than brute force. The card-reading ritual associated with the
valley (see [Madam Eva](/npcs/madam-eva)) is the traditional way to randomize
where his weakness lies and where the final confrontation should fall, so that no
two tellings of his defeat are the same.
