Victor Mordenheim is the natural philosopher at the heart of
[Lamordia](/domains/lamordia)'s horror: a gifted, obsessive scientist who
succeeded at the one experiment he should have left alone. Working in isolation,
he assembled and animated a living being — and then could not bear what he had
made.

## Maker and disowner

Mordenheim's tragedy is one of responsibility refused. He gave [Adam](/darklords/adam)
life and then withheld everything else — acceptance, kinship, a name freely
given. Whether he is the domain's true prisoner or merely the author of its
misery is a question the setting has answered differently over time, but his
guilt and his creation's loneliness are two faces of the same wound.

## A rationalist undone

He embodies Lamordia's central irony: a man of pure reason who produced something
his reason cannot explain away, and who spends the rest of his life failing to
account for it.
