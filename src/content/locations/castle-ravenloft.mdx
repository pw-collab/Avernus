Castle Ravenloft is the seat of [Strahd von Zarovich](/darklords/strahd-von-zarovich),
raised on a spur of rock above the valley of [Barovia](/domains/barovia) so that
its towers are visible from much of the land below. It gave the setting its name,
and it remains the archetypal haunted castle of the game: sprawling, half-ruined,
and dangerous in ways that have little to do with its physical layout.

## A castle that watches back

The fortress is a maze of halls, chapels, crypts, spires, and drops, connected by
stairs and passages that do not always lead where a visitor expects. Much of it is
decayed or abandoned, patrolled by the lord's servants and echoing with the sounds
of a household that has not truly lived for a very long time. It rewards caution:
the danger is rarely a straightforward ambush and more often a slow tightening of
the master's attention as intruders climb toward him.

## The heart of the valley's evil

The castle is where the valley's curse is most concentrated. Its lord treats it as
both home and trap — a place to receive guests with icy hospitality and to let
them exhaust themselves against its corridors before he chooses to act.

<SpoilerWrapper level="dm-only" summary="What waits in the depths">

The lower reaches hold the lord's resting place and the crypts of his line, and it
is here that a final confrontation traditionally falls — though the card-reading
ritual tied to the valley can move that confrontation elsewhere. The castle is
best run as an expression of its master: patient, theatrical, and certain of its
advantage, closing around intruders rather than simply blocking them.
